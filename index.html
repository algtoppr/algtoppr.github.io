About

We are an algebraic topology conference series meeting every two years in San Juan, Puerto Rico, organized by Mona Merling, Jeremy Miller, Manuel Rivera, and Natalia Pacheco Tallaj. The second iteration of this conference will occur on June 5 - June 14, 2024. The conference will focus on recent developments in algebraic topology and their applications to geometry, physics, and data science. We will have talks concerning a wide variety of topics, including stable and chromatic homotopy theory, K-theory, higher category theory, higher algebra, derived geometry, operads, homological stability, configuration spaces, string topology, and topological data analysis. The overarching theme will be the use of algebra to give structure to geometric contexts. We hope that by bringing together a diverse cohort of mathematicians working on different sub-fields in algebraic topology we can foster new ideas and perspectives.

Information for the 2024 conference will be available on this site soon.
